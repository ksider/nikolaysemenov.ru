Summary

Polymer scientist and R&D engineer with 18 years of experience developing materials that are difficult to formulate, difficult to process, and often considered impractical until they work.

My work has ranged from electrorheological suspensions, elastomers, silicone insulators, and wood–polymer composites to biodegradable food-contact packaging based on natural fillers. Regardless of the material system, the challenge has always been the same: understanding why a material behaves the way it does, finding practical solutions, and turning laboratory concepts into reliable manufacturing processes.

Over the years, I've built laboratories from the ground up, established and led multidisciplinary R&D teams, developed new formulations, commissioned production equipment, supported technology transfer, and worked closely with manufacturing, customers, and research partners. My background combines polymer physics, rheology, materials science, and industrial process development, allowing me to move comfortably between laboratory research and factory floors.

In recent years, my work has also included regulatory aspects of sustainable food-contact materials, including compliance with EU food-contact legislation, PPWR, and practical implementation of material qualification and documentation for industrial and customer validation.

This website is a collection of projects I've worked on, lessons learned along the way, and the people who made those projects possible.

Bpacks Lab · 2023–2026

Stabilizing the extrusion window

Context

Continuous extrusion of moisture-sensitive, fiber-filled biobased compounds is prone to melt fracture, die drool, and inconsistent gauge whenever the process window drifts.

Result

Tightened the temperature profile and screw-speed settings for the specific filler loadings used in the Bpacks formulations, cutting defect rates and delivering consistent output batch-to-batch.

Bpacks Lab · 2023–2026

Removing short-shots and warpage

Context

Filled biobased compounds are unforgiving in injection molding — narrow processing windows show up as short-shots, sink marks, and warpage.

Result

Reworked gate, cooling, and packing-pressure settings for the affected tools, cutting defect rates and enabling reliable, repeatable output on the production tool.

Bpacks Lab · 2023–2026

Getting clean draws in thermoforming

Context

Thermoforming rigid trays and lids from fiber-filled biobased sheet is sensitive to sheet-temperature uniformity — uneven heating shows up as thin spots, webbing, or poor detail in the finished part.

Result

Adjusted sheet pre-heat and forming-cycle parameters to the specific sheet formulation, reducing defect rates and stabilizing part quality across runs.
